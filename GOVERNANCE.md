# Working Group & Governance

*How this open standard is built and decided. To be ratified at Session 1.*

## Who we're convening
Roboticists · safety & functional-safety engineers · standards-body members (ISO/TC 299, IEC/SC 65A) · regulators & public-safety/law-enforcement experts · AI-governance leaders · ethicists · insurers & liability experts. Convenor: **Cognita GRC** (steward, not owner).

## Principles
- **Open by default** — text CC BY 4.0, API spec Apache-2.0; anyone may implement.
- **Rough consensus** — decisions by working-group consensus; the convenor facilitates, does not dictate.
- **Evidence over opinion** — proposals cite standards, incidents, or data.
- **Public-safety first** — where in doubt, the safer, more human-controlled, more provable option wins.

## How to participate
1. **Join** — open an Issue titled `Join: <role>` (one line on your background).
2. **Comment** — open Issues against `FORCE-CONTINUUM.md`, `EMBODIED-AI-GOVERNANCE.md`, or `GOVERNANCE-API.md`.
3. **Propose** — PRs for substantive change require a linked Issue + rationale.

## Decision flow
Draft → RFC (Issue open ≥ 2 weeks) → working-group review → merge on consensus → versioned release (`v0.1 → v0.2 → …`). Material safety decisions require sign-off from ≥ 2 functional-safety and ≥ 1 regulatory participant.

## First session — proposed agenda
1. Ratify scope + principles.
2. **Force Continuum v0.1** — adopt/refine the PAFC levels + Force–Control Coupling as the first RFC.
3. Assign leads per document.
4. Decide the conformance/attestation model (self vs. third-party) and the minimal audit-log schema.
5. Set cadence + the v0.2 milestone.

## Roadmap
- **v0.1** (now) — drafts published, call for participants.
- **v0.2** — Force Continuum RFC ratified; minimal audit-log schema fixed; reference API OpenAPI spec.
- **v0.3** — pilot with ≥ 1 robotics vendor; map to EU MR 2023/1230 conformity route + EU AI Act.
- **v1.0** — citable standard + open reference implementation.

*Not legal advice or a certification scheme; a community standard designed to complement ISO 10218:2025, ISO 13482, the EU Machinery Regulation 2023/1230, the EU AI Act, and ISO/IEC 42001.*
